2022 Paper - Related rates for a bowl of water

Question

When the height of the water level in the bowl is \(h\) cm, the volume, \(V\) cm\(^3\), of water in the bowl is given by

\[ V=\pi\left(\frac{3}{2}h^2+3h\right). \]

Water is poured into the bowl at a constant rate of \(20\text{ cm}^3\text{ s}^{-1}\).

\[ \text{Find the rate, in cm s}^{-1}\text{, at which the height of the water level is increasing when } h=3\text{ cm.} \]

You must use calculus and show any derivatives that you need when solving this problem.

Try the question yourself first. If you get stuck, open the hints before using the full walkthrough.

Step 1

Identify the given rate

Which derivative is given directly in the question?

Step 2

Differentiate the volume formula

Find \(\frac{dV}{dh}\) from the given expression for \(V\).

Step 3

Link the rates

Use the chain rule for related rates.

Step 4

Solve for \(\frac{dh}{dt}\)

Rearrange and then substitute \(h=3\).

\[ 20=(3\pi h+3\pi)\frac{dh}{dt} \] \[ \frac{dh}{dt}=\frac{20}{3\pi h+3\pi} \] \[ \frac{dh}{dt}=\frac{20}{3\pi(3)+3\pi} \] \[ \frac{dh}{dt}=\frac{20}{12\pi} \] \[ \frac{dh}{dt}=\frac{5}{3\pi} \]
Great work. The height of the water is increasing at \(\frac{5}{3\pi}\text{ cm s}^{-1}\).
