Integration Techniques NCEA practice questions

Practise choosing and preparing an integration method from the structure of the integrand or from tabulated numerical data.

← All skills

Skill overview

What these questions practise

The matched methods explicitly include reverse-chain patterns, substitution, trigonometric identities, algebraic rewriting, logarithmic forms, and the Trapezium or Simpson's Rule.

Relevant standards: NCEA Level 3 Integration — AS91579

This collection contains 49 matching walkthroughs across 9 examination years.

The grouping is based on the method recorded for each walkthrough. Use the linked NZQA standard and paper material for authoritative wording, diagrams, and assessment information.

Before you start

Common mistakes in these methods

  • Check the derivative of the inner expression and account for any constant factor when reversing a chain-rule pattern.
  • Transform the full integrand under the substitution, including the differential and any definite-integral limits.
  • Apply the trigonometric identity before integrating and preserve its constant factors.
