Question

\[ f(x)=2x^3-3x^2+\frac{x}{2}+7 \] \[ \text{Use calculus to find the gradient of the function at } x=-\frac{1}{2}. \]

Try the question yourself first. If you get stuck, open the hints before using the full walkthrough.

Step 1

Choose the right method

What is the best way to start differentiating this function?

Step 2

Differentiate the cubic term

What is the derivative of \(2x^3\)?

Step 3

Differentiate the rest of the function

What is the derivative of \(-3x^2+\frac{x}{2}+7\)?

Step 4

Write the full derivative

Putting the terms together, which expression is \(f'(x)\)?

Step 5

Substitute the given x-value

Which substitution correctly finds the gradient at \(x=-\frac{1}{2}\)?

Step 6

Final answer

Now simplify the substitution carefully.

\[ f'(x)=6x^2-6x+\frac{1}{2} \] \[ f'\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)=6\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)-6\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)+\frac{1}{2} \] \[ =\frac{6}{4}+3+\frac{1}{2} \] \[ =\frac{3}{2}+3+\frac{1}{2}=5 \]
Great work. The gradient at \(x=-\frac{1}{2}\) is \(5\).
