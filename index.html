Browse papers by level and year

This site provides interactive walkthroughs for NCEA Calculus papers. Each question is broken into small steps to help you understand the reasoning behind the solution, not just the final answer.

Work through each step, read the feedback, and move to the next stage once you understand the idea. The goal is to help you build the thinking required for Achieved, Merit, and Excellence answers.

Level 2 Calculus

2025 Paper

This section is reserved for the 2025 Level 2 Calculus paper so it stays separate from the Level 3 walkthroughs below.

Question 1

Question 1(a) Focus: using the power rule term by term and evaluating the gradient at a point. Question 1(b) Focus: finding the point of tangency, the gradient, and the tangent equation. Question 1(c) Focus: finding critical points, identifying the minimum, and solving for the constant. Question 1(d) Focus: writing displacement equations, solving when they are equal, and answering in context.

Question 2

Question 2(a) Focus: sketching the derivative from stationary points, signs, and changes in concavity. Question 2(b) Focus: antidifferentiating and using a point on the graph to find the constant. Question 2(c) Focus: differentiating, evaluating a rate, and using derivatives to justify the minimum turning point. Question 2(d) Focus: using a stationary point and an x-intercept to find constants, then classifying the other stationary point.

Question 3

Question 3(a) Focus: differentiating, solving for the given gradient, and finding the coordinate on the curve. Question 3(b) Focus: forming an area expression, finding the height from the given area, and evaluating the rate of change. Question 3(c) Focus: using a surface-area constraint, writing volume in one variable, maximizing it, and proving the maximum. Question 3(d) Focus: differentiating, factorising, classifying critical points, and finding where the function is decreasing.

Level 3 Calculus

2022 Paper

This walkthrough set is for the 2022 Level 3 Calculus paper. Choose a walkthrough to work through.

Question 1

Question 1(a) Focus: differentiation of the function. Question 1(b) Focus: finding and interpreting stationary points. Question 1(c) Focus: the normal line and its x-intercept. Question 1(d) Focus: parametric differentiation. Question 1(e) Focus: proving there are no points of inflection.

Question 2

Question 2(a) Focus: product rule with a trig chain rule. Question 2(b) Focus: chain rule and finding a tangent gradient. Question 2(c) Focus: quotient rule and finding when motion is stationary. Question 2(d) Focus: forming an area function and maximising it. Question 2(e) Focus: implicit differentiation and tangent-line distance.

Question 3

Question 3(a) Focus: rewriting radicals and using the chain rule. Question 3(b) Focus: reading differentiability, derivatives, and limits from a graph. Question 3(c) Focus: related rates using volume and height. Question 3(d) Focus: finding stationary points and classifying them. Question 3(e) Focus: building a time function and minimising it.
