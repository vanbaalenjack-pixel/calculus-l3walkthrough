This site provides interactive walkthroughs of the NCEA Level 3 Calculus exam. Each question is broken into small steps to help you understand the reasoning behind the solution, not just the final answer.

Work through each step, read the feedback, and move to the next stage once you understand the idea. The goal is to help you build the thinking required for Achieved, Merit, and Excellence answers.

2022 Paper

This walkthrough set is for Standard 91578 Differentiation. Start with the 2022 paper and choose a walkthrough to work through.

Question 1

Question 1(a) Focus: differentiation of the function. Question 1(b) Focus: finding and interpreting stationary points. Question 1(c) Focus: the normal line and its x-intercept. Question 1(d) Focus: parametric differentiation. Question 1(e) Focus: proving there are no points of inflection.

Question 2

Question 2(a) Focus: product rule with a trig chain rule. Question 2(b) Focus: chain rule and finding a tangent gradient. Question 2(c) Focus: quotient rule and finding when motion is stationary. Question 2(d) Focus: forming an area function and maximising it. Question 2(e) Focus: implicit differentiation and tangent-line distance.

Question 3

Question 3(a) Focus: rewriting radicals and using the chain rule. Question 3(b) Focus: reading differentiability, derivatives, and limits from a graph. Question 3(c) Focus: related rates using volume and height. Question 3(d) Focus: finding stationary points and classifying them. Question 3(e) Focus: building a time function and minimising it.
