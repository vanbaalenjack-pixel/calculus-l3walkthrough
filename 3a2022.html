Question

\[ \text{Differentiate } y=e^{4\sqrt{x}}. \]

You do not need to simplify your answer.

Try the question yourself first. If you get stuck, open the hints before using the full walkthrough.

Step 1

Rewrite the radical as a power

This makes the inside function easier to differentiate.

Step 2

Use the chain rule

Differentiate the exponential, then multiply by the derivative of the exponent.

Preview will appear here.

Step 3

Final answer

A correct derivative is:

\[ \frac{dy}{dx}=e^{4\sqrt{x}}\cdot \frac{2}{\sqrt{x}} \]

This could also be written as \(\frac{2e^{4\sqrt{x}}}{\sqrt{x}}\), but simplification is optional here.

Learning summary

Review the method, not only the answer

This walkthrough focuses on rewriting radicals and using the chain rule. Afterwards, try the same method again without hints and explain the reasoning that connects each step.

Common mistake to avoid

Do not stop after differentiating the outside function; include the derivative of the inside function as a factor.
