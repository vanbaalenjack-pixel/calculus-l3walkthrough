Question

Hemi is part of a student research project on how caffeine enters the bloodstream. A new device measures caffeine levels in his blood.

He is monitored for 180 minutes (3 hours) and, at some point, he is given a cup of coffee to drink.

\[ C(t)=\frac{t^3}{150}-1.4t^2+80t+240 \qquad \{0 \le t \le 180\} \] \[ \text{where }C\text{ is the concentration }(\mu\text{g/L}),\text{ and }t\text{ is time in minutes.} \] \[ \text{(i) Show that the rate of change of concentration is }-16\text{ after }60\text{ minutes.} \] \[ \text{(ii) Use calculus to justify when Hemi was given the coffee.} \]

Now write both parts clearly.

\[ C'(t)=\frac{t^2}{50}-2.8t+80 \] \[ C'(60)=\frac{60^2}{50}-2.8(60)+80=72-168+80=-16 \] \[ C'(t)=0 \Rightarrow \frac{t^2}{50}-2.8t+80=0 \] \[ t^2-140t+4000=0 \] \[ (t-40)(t-100)=0 \] \[ C''(t)=\frac{t}{25}-2.8 \] \[ C''(40)=-1.2 \text{ and } C''(100)=1.2 \] \[ \text{So the minimum turning point is at } t=100 \] \[ \text{Hemi was given the coffee at }100\text{ minutes.} \]
Great work. The rate at 60 minutes is \(-16\), and the calculus model shows the coffee was given at 100 minutes.
