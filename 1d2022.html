Question

A curve is defined parametrically by the equations:

\[ x=2+3t \qquad \text{and} \qquad y=3t-\ln(3t-1), \qquad t>\frac{1}{3} \]

Find the coordinates, \((x,y)\), of any point(s) on the curve where the tangent to the curve has a gradient of \(\frac{1}{2}\).

Try the question yourself first. If you get stuck, open the hints before using the full walkthrough.

You must use calculus and show any derivatives that you need.

Step 1

Find \(\frac{dx}{dt}\)

Differentiate \(x=2+3t\) with respect to \(t\).

Preview will appear here.

Step 2

Find \(\frac{dy}{dt}\)

Differentiate \(y=3t-\ln(3t-1)\) with respect to \(t\).

Preview will appear here.

Step 3

Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\)

For parametric equations, multiply \(\frac{dy}{dt}\) by \(\frac{dt}{dx}\) so you can see the \(dt\) cancelling.

Preview will appear here.

Step 4

Set the gradient equal to \(\frac{1}{2}\)

Solve for \(t\) when the tangent gradient is \(\frac{1}{2}\).

\[ 1-\frac{1}{3t-1}=\frac{1}{2} \]
Preview will appear here.

Step 5

Find the coordinates

Substitute \(t=1\) into both parametric equations.

Preview will appear here.

Step 6

Final answer and graph

There is one point on the curve where the tangent gradient is \(\frac{1}{2}\).

\[ (x,y)=\left(5,\ 3-\ln 2\right) \]
