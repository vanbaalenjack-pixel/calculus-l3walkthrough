Stationary Points and Optimisation NCEA practice questions

Practise finding and classifying stationary or critical points, then using derivative evidence to maximise or minimise a model.

← All skills

Skill overview

What these questions practise

The matched Level 2 and Level 3 methods include derivative sign, first- and second-derivative classification, graph interpretation, and optimisation in geometric or contextual domains.

Relevant standards: NCEA Level 2 Calculus — AS91262 · NCEA Level 3 Differentiation — AS91578

This collection contains 43 matching walkthroughs across 10 examination years.

The grouping is based on the method recorded for each walkthrough. Use the linked NZQA standard and paper material for authoritative wording, diagrams, and assessment information.

Before you start

Common mistakes in these methods

  • Solving the derivative condition finds a candidate; use the original function when a coordinate is also required.
  • Do not label a maximum, minimum, or stationary inflection without the sign or derivative evidence requested by the question.
  • Check the model's domain and interpret the admissible optimum in context rather than reporting every algebraic candidate.
